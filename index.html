Why We Fast

Fasting isn’t about skipping meals or being extra spiritual. It’s about making space for God and removing distractions so we can hear Him more clearly. We’re not fasting to get something from God—we’re fasting to give Him our first and best.

Types of Fasts

Complete Fast

A fast from all solid foods, consuming only liquids such as water, light juices, or broths.

Selective Fast (Daniel Fast)

Removing certain foods like meat, sweets, or processed foods. Focuses on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and water.

Partial Fast

Fasting during certain times of the day, such as skipping breakfast and lunch.

Soul Fast (Everyone Is Called to This)

Stepping away from digital and emotional distractions such as social media, streaming, news, or anything that steals focus. Replace that time with prayer, Scripture, worship, and silence.

From This Week’s Teaching

We talked about Practical Prayer and the discipline of prayer. One of the most important things we said was this:

Prayer is learned. Prayer is practiced. Prayer is proven.

The disciples didn’t say, “Teach us how to preach.” They said, “Lord, teach us to pray.”

If prayer feels awkward, inconsistent, or unfamiliar to you, you’re in good company. Prayer grows the same way relationships do—one conversation at a time.

A Simple Way to Practice Prayer

Pick one time, one place, and one simple prayer:

  • One time: when you wake up, in the car, on a lunch break, before bed
  • One place: the kitchen, the driveway, the shower, your truck
  • One simple prayer: “God, I’m here.” “God, help me today.” “God, let Your will shape mine.”

When you think to pray—pray. It doesn’t have to be long. It just has to be real.

Week Two: WORK

Jesus in what we do, not just where we go.

Day 8: Purpose

Colossians 3:23 — “Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart...”

God can use every bit of what you do—work, home, or school—as part of His kingdom. He’s not just calling pastors. He’s calling you.

Prayer:

Help me serve You in everything I do.

Action:

Ask God to show you the purpose in your current season.

Day 9: Stewardship

1 Corinthians 4:2

God entrusts us with time, energy, and influence. Stewarding well doesn’t mean doing more—it means aligning your life with what matters most.

Prayer:

Teach me to steward my time, talents, and tasks well.

Action:

Take inventory of where your time is going this week.

Day 10: Influence

Matthew 5:13

You may never hold a microphone, but your life speaks. Influence is less about platform and more about presence. Let others see Jesus in you.

Prayer:

Use me to influence others for Your glory.

Action:

Encourage or bless someone in your workplace or school.

Day 11: Joy in the Doing

Ecclesiastes 3:12–13

There’s joy in the ordinary. You don’t need a dream job to live on purpose. Ask God to meet you in the middle of your normal.

Prayer:

Restore joy in my daily responsibilities.

Action:

Name one thing you enjoy about your work or daily tasks.

Day 12: Provision

Philippians 4:19

Provision isn’t always money. God provides peace, strength, and wisdom too. He is your source—not your paycheck.

Prayer:

I trust You to meet my needs.

Action:

Thank God for one recent way He’s provided.

Day 13: Wisdom

James 1:5

God gives wisdom freely. You don’t have to have all the answers—just ask Him. Wisdom is available for every decision, big or small.

Prayer:

Give me wisdom for today’s decisions.

Action:

Ask the Holy Spirit to lead one choice today.

Day 14: Rest

Exodus 20:8

Rest is not laziness. It’s worship. When you rest, you’re saying: God, I trust You more than my schedule.

Prayer:

Help me honor rest as part of obedience.

Action:

Schedule intentional time to rest this weekend.
