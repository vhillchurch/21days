A Message from Pastor Mason

Victory Hill Family,

As we wrap up our first week of 21 Days of Prayer and Fasting, I just want to pause and say this to you: I’m proud of you.

Some of you jumped in ready. Some eased in slowly. Some are still figuring it out. You're not behind—you’re right where God can meet you.

This year, we added a soul fast: stepping away from social media to create space to listen, breathe, and pray.

God is not asking you for perfection. He’s inviting you into connection.

Prayer is learned. Prayer is practiced. Prayer is proven.

If prayer feels awkward or inconsistent, you're not alone. Just keep showing up.

Simple prayer rhythm:

  • One time: when you wake up, in the car, at lunch
  • One place: the kitchen, driveway, or shower
  • One prayer: “God, I’m here.” or “God, help me today.”

Fasting isn’t about what you’re giving up—it’s about what you’re making room for. And God meets us in the showing up.

Either God will move the situation, or God will move you. Both are gifts.

With you and for you,
Pastor Mason

Why We Fast

Fasting is not about skipping meals or earning something from God. It’s about creating space to hear Him more clearly and giving Him our first and best.

Types of Fasts

Complete Fast

Only liquids such as water, juice, or broths.

Selective Fast (Daniel Fast)

Remove sweets, meats, or processed food. Eat fruits, vegetables, and grains.

Partial Fast

Fast during part of the day, like skipping lunch or sunup to sundown.

Soul Fast

Everyone is encouraged to fast from digital distractions and lean into Scripture, prayer, and worship.
